For the Shark PowerDetect robot series, I worked on the mopping system, drive wheel traction, and front sensor suite packaging.

---

### Mopping System

My contributions included:

- Water distribution manifold and pad attachment plate to ensure even coverage for the pad wash feature
- Microfiber pad material sourcing and testing to deliver an antimicrobial coating solution for a front-of-box claim
- Microbial growth mitigation research and testing for dirty mop water stored on the dock
- Test protocol design and management of US and China-based test teams to determine system requirements for performance, square foot coverage, and packaging KPIs, including:
  - Robot and dock water tank capacities
  - Robot and dock water and air pump software logic for pad wash feature and during mission
  - Pad downforce, pressure distribution, and agitation RPM

These designs factored in test data, system tolerance stacks, and consumer study feedback. This work delivered the best mopping robot on the market across 4000 sqft.

---

### Drive Wheel Traction

My contributions included:

- Test protocol design to quantify wheel slip from delta between LiDAR and wheel encoder data
- Tread design testing
- Optimizing drag force from mop pad with ballast distribution and attachment plate spring load
- Optimizing acceleration profile

This work resulted in a 25% reduction in robot mopping navigation efficiency (sqft/min) and a 30% reduction in stuck scenario failure modes during mission.

---

### Front Sensor Suite Packaging

My contributions included:

- Bumper shroud to reduce lens dust accumulation
- Lens geometry changes based on consumer feedback
- DFA adding registration surfaces and bolted joints
